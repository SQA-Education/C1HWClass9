Planning Around U.S. Holidays

The U.S. has several major national holidays, each with its own cultural meaning and schedule. Some occur on fixed dates, such as Juneteenth on June 19 and Independence Day on July 4.

Others are movable dates. For example, Martin Luther King Jr. Day falls on the third Monday in January, while Memorial Day is observed on the last Monday in May.

When discussing holidays professionally, advanced time expressions matter. People may refer to the day before Thanksgiving, the weekend following Labor Day, or events that happen annually, biannually, or biennially. Knowing these expressions helps you communicate schedules clearly at work and in daily life.
